About Gene Fusions

Gene fusions result from chromosomal rearrangements that join parts of two different genes. They are important in cancer because:

  • Oncogenic drivers: Many fusions create oncoproteins (e.g., BCR-ABL1)
  • Drug targets: Fusion proteins can be targeted by specific inhibitors
  • Diagnostic markers: Certain fusions define cancer subtypes
  • Prognostic value: Fusion status can predict patient outcomes
